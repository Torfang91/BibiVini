2021 Felix's Old Vine Zinfandel

Heritage Vines, Modern Excellence

Wine Details

Vintage: 2021

Alcohol: 14.8%

pH: 3.65

TA: 6.2 g/L

Residual Sugar: 0.8 g/L (Dry)

Production: 485 cases

Bottle Price: $48

Winemaking

Harvest Date: October 12, 2021

Barrel Aging: 16 months

Oak: 40% new French oak, 60% neutral

Toast Level: Medium+

Fermentation: Native yeast, concrete tanks

Bottling Date: February 18, 2023

Tasting Notes

Appearance Deep ruby with violet edges. Medium+ viscosity showing glycerin legs that slowly cascade down the glass.
Nose Explosive aromatics of wild blackberry, sun-ripened raspberry, and black cherry compote. Secondary notes of dried herbs, white pepper, and sweet baking spices with hints of tobacco and leather.
Palate Full-bodied with velvety tannins. Rich dark fruit core balanced by bright acidity. Flavors of blackberry jam, plum, dark chocolate, and espresso with savory undertones of cured meat and Mediterranean herbs.
Finish Long and persistent. Lingering notes of black pepper, cocoa, and ripe fruit. The old vine concentration shows through with remarkable depth and complexity.

Food Pairing

This bold Zinfandel pairs beautifully with:

Grilled Meats BBQ ribs, smoked brisket, grilled lamb chops, or bacon-wrapped filet mignon
Italian Classics Lasagna, osso buco, wild boar ragu, or aged Parmigiano-Reggiano
Bold Flavors Spicy sausage pizza, blackened salmon, or mushroom risotto with truffle oil

Vintage Notes

The 2021 growing season in Dry Creek Valley was marked by a warm spring and moderate summer temperatures, allowing for even ripening. A brief heat spike in early October concentrated flavors before harvest. These old vines, planted in 1947, produced intensely flavored, small-clustered fruit with exceptional concentration and balance.

Drinking Window

Peak: 2024-2028
Can be enjoyed now with decanting, or cellared for continued development through 2030.
