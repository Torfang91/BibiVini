2022 Gaffer's Grenache

Bright, Fruit-Forward, and Silky

Wine Details

Vintage: 2022

Alcohol: 14.6%

pH: 3.75

TA: 5.5 g/L

Residual Sugar: 0.8 g/L (Dry)

Production: 340 cases

Bottle Price: $45

Winemaking

Harvest Date: September 24, 2022

Barrel Aging: 15 months

Oak: 30% new French oak, 70% neutral

Toast Level: Light-Medium

Fermentation: Native yeast, open-top fermenters

Bottling Date: December 20, 2023

Tasting Notes

Appearance Deep ruby-garnet with brilliant clarity. Medium+ body with prominent legs indicating ripe fruit concentration.
Nose Intensely aromatic with ripe strawberry jam, cherry compote, and raspberry liqueur. Warm vintage character shows through with notes of dried fig, cinnamon, and star anise. Hints of Mediterranean herbs and garrigue add savory depth.
Palate Rich and lush with velvety tannins. Concentrated red fruit flavors of strawberry, cherry, and red plum dominate. Mid-palate reveals layers of warm spice, cocoa, and toasted almond. The warm vintage produces generous fruit with supple texture.
Finish Long and expressive with persistent fruit and spice. Notes of dried flowers and sweet oak linger. The wine's richness is balanced by integrated acidity, showcasing Grenache's ability to shine in warmer vintages.

Food Pairing

This elegant Grenache pairs beautifully with:

Mediterranean Cuisine Herb-crusted lamb, ratatouille, grilled vegetable platters, or Provençal chicken
Charcuterie & Cheese Prosciutto, salami, aged Manchego, or soft-ripened cheeses
Light & Fresh Duck breast with cherry reduction, mushroom tart, or tuna niçoise salad

Vintage Notes

2022 was a warm, early vintage that favored Grenache. The drought-tolerant 1103P rootstock performed exceptionally well, allowing the vines to thrive despite limited water. Early harvest captured optimal phenolic ripeness while preserving freshness. Gamgee's Block, at 850 feet elevation, benefited from cool evening breezes that maintained acidity in the ripe fruit.

Drinking Window

Peak: 2025-2029
Delicious now and will evolve gracefully through 2031.
