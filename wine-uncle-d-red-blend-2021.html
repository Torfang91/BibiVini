2021 Uncle D's Red Blend

Rich, Smooth, and Approachable

Wine Details

Vintage: 2021

Blend: 45% Cabernet Sauvignon, 30% Merlot, 15% Syrah, 10% Petite Sirah

Alcohol: 14.2%

pH: 3.68

TA: 6.0 g/L

Residual Sugar: 0.8 g/L (Dry)

Production: 750 cases

Bottle Price: $32

Winemaking

Harvest Date: October 5-15, 2021

Barrel Aging: 14 months

Oak: 30% new French & American oak, 70% neutral

Toast Level: Medium

Fermentation: Separately fermented, blended before aging

Bottling Date: December 8, 2022

Tasting Notes

Appearance Deep ruby-purple with garnet edges. Full-bodied with pronounced legs reflecting the wine's richness and smooth texture.
Nose Inviting aromatics of blackberry, dark cherry, and plum. Notes of vanilla, mocha, and sweet oak intermingle with hints of violet, black pepper, and dark chocolate. Subtle touches of leather and dried herbs add complexity.
Palate Rich and smooth with supple tannins. Flavors of blackberry, dark cherry, and plum dominate, complemented by notes of cocoa, vanilla, and toasted oak. Mid-palate reveals layers of espresso, black pepper, and subtle spice. The blend creates a harmonious wine with excellent balance.
Finish Long and satisfying with lingering dark fruit and oak spice. Smooth tannins and integrated acidity make this an incredibly approachable and food-friendly wine. Perfect for everyday enjoyment or special occasions.

Food Pairing

This versatile red blend pairs beautifully with:

Grilled & BBQ Grilled steak, BBQ ribs, burgers, lamb chops, or smoked brisket
Italian Favorites Pasta Bolognese, lasagna, osso buco, or margherita pizza
Comfort Food Pot roast, meatloaf, shepherd's pie, or aged cheddar

Vintage Notes

The 2021 vintage provided excellent conditions across all varietals in this blend. The Cabernet Sauvignon from Alexander Valley provides structure and dark fruit, while Russian River Merlot adds softness and plush texture. Dry Creek Syrah contributes peppery spice and savory notes, and a touch of Petite Sirah deepens color and adds backbone. This vintage showcases the art of blending multiple varietals and appellations into a harmonious whole.

Drinking Window

Peak: 2024-2028
Drinking beautifully now with its smooth texture and approachable style.
