2022 Que Sarah Syrah

Bold, Peppery, and Structured

Wine Details

Vintage: 2022

Alcohol: 15.1%

pH: 3.75

TA: 5.6 g/L

Residual Sugar: 1.0 g/L (Dry)

Production: 580 cases

Bottle Price: $48

Winemaking

Harvest Date: October 2, 2022

Barrel Aging: 20 months

Oak: 55% new French oak, 45% neutral

Toast Level: Medium+

Fermentation: Extended maceration, 10% whole cluster

Bottling Date: June 8, 2024

Tasting Notes

Appearance Inky, impenetrable purple-black with garnet edges. Massive body with thick, syrupy legs indicating exceptional concentration.
Nose Explosively aromatic with ripe blackberry jam, blueberry compote, and black plum. Intense notes of black pepper, smoked bacon, and grilled meat. Layers of dark chocolate, espresso, vanilla, and charred oak with hints of tar and leather.
Palate Massively structured with bold, powerful tannins. Opulent flavors of blackberry liqueur, blueberry pie, and dark plum dominate. Mid-palate explodes with black pepper, smoked meat, dark chocolate, and toasted oak. The warm vintage produces a wine of tremendous concentration and intensity.
Finish Extraordinarily long with wave after wave of dark fruit, pepper, and savory spice. Firm tannins suggest decades of aging potential. This is a monumental Syrah showcasing the power possible in warm vintages while maintaining balance.

Food Pairing

This bold Syrah pairs beautifully with:

Grilled & Smoked Meats BBQ brisket, grilled lamb chops, smoked ribs, or pepper-crusted steak
Rich & Savory Beef bourguignon, venison stew, braised short ribs, or duck confit
Mediterranean Flavors Lamb tagine, grilled sausages, cassoulet, or aged Gouda

Vintage Notes

2022 was an exceptional warm vintage in Dry Creek Valley that produced intensely concentrated Syrah. Consistent heat through the growing season created small berries with thick skins and exceptional phenolic ripeness. Early harvest captured peak ripeness while preserving some acidity. This vintage represents the power and intensity possible from this site in warm years, producing a wine of remarkable depth and aging potential.

Drinking Window

Peak: 2028-2037
Best cellared for at least 5 years, with potential to age gracefully through 2042.
