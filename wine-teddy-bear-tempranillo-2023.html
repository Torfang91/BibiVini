2023 Teddy Bear Tempranillo

Earthy, Food-Friendly, and Balanced

Wine Details

Vintage: 2023

Alcohol: 13.5%

pH: 3.58

TA: 6.8 g/L

Residual Sugar: 0.4 g/L (Dry)

Production: 375 cases

Bottle Price: $40

Winemaking

Harvest Date: October 18, 2023

Barrel Aging: 15 months

Oak: 35% new American oak, 65% neutral

Toast Level: Light-Medium

Fermentation: Traditional fermentation, 26 days on skins

Bottling Date: January 20, 2025

Tasting Notes

Appearance Brilliant ruby-garnet with orange-brick rim. Medium body with elegant legs reflecting the wine's balanced, classic structure.
Nose Elegant aromatics of red cherry, wild strawberry, and cranberry. Notes of dried rose petals, tea leaves, and earthy leather. Subtle hints of vanilla, cedar, dried herbs, and tobacco with mineral undertones and forest floor complexity.
Palate Medium-bodied with fine-grained, silky tannins and vibrant acidity. Flavors of red cherry, pomegranate, and red plum are complemented by savory notes of leather, tobacco, and dried herbs. Mid-palate reveals subtle oak spice, tea, and earthy minerality. The cooler vintage produces exceptional freshness and elegance.
Finish Clean and refreshing with lingering red fruit and earthy notes. Delicate spice, leather, and mineral nuances persist. This vintage exemplifies classic Tempranillo elegance with remarkable balance and food-friendliness. A wine of purity and restraint.

Food Pairing

This versatile Tempranillo pairs beautifully with:

Spanish Classics Paella, chorizo, Manchego cheese, jamón ibérico, or patatas bravas
Grilled & Roasted Lamb chops, roasted pork loin, grilled vegetables, or herb-crusted chicken
Comfort Food Pasta with meat sauce, pizza, burgers with aged cheddar, or mushroom risotto

Vintage Notes

The 2023 vintage was characterized by cooler temperatures and well-timed rainfall, creating ideal conditions for elegant Tempranillo. The extended growing season allowed for gradual flavor development while preserving vibrant natural acidity and traditional earthy character. Later harvest captured optimal phenolic maturity with moderate sugar levels. This vintage showcases the variety's Old World elegance adapted to California terroir.

Drinking Window

Peak: 2026-2031
Approachable now but the structure and acidity suggest excellent aging potential through 2034.
