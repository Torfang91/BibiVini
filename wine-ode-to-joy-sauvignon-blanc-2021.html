2021 Ode to Joy Sauvignon Blanc

Crisp, Vibrant, and Refreshing

Wine Details

Vintage: 2021

Alcohol: 13.2%

pH: 3.28

TA: 7.2 g/L

Residual Sugar: 1.8 g/L (Dry)

Production: 380 cases

Bottle Price: $28

Winemaking

Harvest Date: September 8, 2021

Aging: 5 months

Vessel: 85% stainless steel, 15% neutral French oak

Lees Contact: Sur lie aging for texture

Fermentation: Cool fermentation, selected yeast

Bottling Date: February 10, 2022

Tasting Notes

Appearance Pale straw-yellow with greenish highlights. Light to medium body with delicate legs reflecting the wine's crisp, refreshing nature.
Nose Vibrant aromatics of grapefruit, lime zest, and passion fruit. Notes of fresh-cut grass, gooseberry, and white flowers. Subtle hints of jalapeño, lemongrass, and wet stone minerality add complexity.
Palate Bright and lively with crisp acidity. Flavors of Meyer lemon, pink grapefruit, and green apple are complemented by tropical notes of guava and passion fruit. Mid-palate reveals hints of fresh herbs, bell pepper, and a subtle mineral edge. Clean and refreshing throughout.
Finish Clean and zesty with lingering citrus and herbal notes. The bright acidity and mineral undertones create a refreshing, palate-cleansing finish. Classic Russian River Valley Sauvignon Blanc showcasing purity and varietal character.

Food Pairing

This crisp Sauvignon Blanc pairs beautifully with:

Seafood & Shellfish Oysters, grilled fish tacos, ceviche, sushi, or steamed mussels with white wine and herbs
Fresh & Light Goat cheese salad, asparagus with lemon, Caesar salad, or fresh spring rolls
Herbs & Citrus Chicken piccata, herb-crusted halibut, lemon risotto, or pesto pasta

Vintage Notes

The 2021 vintage in Russian River Valley provided ideal conditions for Sauvignon Blanc. Cool mornings and moderate daytime temperatures preserved the wine's natural acidity and aromatic intensity. Early harvest captured vibrant fruit flavors while maintaining freshness. The combination of stainless steel and minimal neutral oak contact adds subtle texture without masking the wine's pure varietal character.

Drinking Window

Peak: 2022-2024
Best enjoyed young to preserve its vibrant aromatics and crisp acidity.
